About this site

The relationship between the United States and Iran has been troubled since the 1979 revolution in Iran, and now is caught in a continuing spiral of suspicions and accusations.  This mutual hostility has many causes in real events going back to the early 1950s and in the national narratives that have grown over the decades.  Remarkably, however, there have also been opportunities to improve the relationship and put the misunderstandings and tensions aside.  Most of these opportunities have been squandered, however, and now seem to be irretrievable.  This project, based on a series of meetings of policy makers and scholars, interviews, and other research, seeks to answer why those opportunities were missed and what the consequences have been.  We cover the period of the Iran-Iraq War, the period of reform in Iran and its prelude, and the increasingly fractious period of the George W. Bush presidency.  Resources include original policy documents, many of them declassified by this project, interviews, journalistic accounts, memoirs, and transcripts of the project meetings, among others.
